Historical Context

The area that is now Pakistan has a history that goes back thousands of years. It was home to one of the world’s oldest civilizations—the Indus Valley Civilization, famous for its advanced cities like Mohenjo-daro and Harappa.

Over time, many empires ruled this region, including the Persians, Greeks, Mughals, and the British. Each of these civilizations left behind architecture, art, and traditions that still influence Pakistan today.

Pakistan became an independent country on August 14, 1947, under the leadership of Muhammad Ali Jinnah. The creation of Pakistan was based on the idea of giving Muslims a separate nation where they could practice their religion and culture freely. Since then, Pakistan has grown into a country with a strong identity, a diverse population, and an important place in South Asian history.
